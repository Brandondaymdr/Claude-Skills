tailored for @carlagentileyoga and @carlagentileday. Covers content pillars,
posting cadence, format strategy, hashtag research, caption writing, and
funnel tactics for growing followers, driving to Circle community, and

## 1. Content Pillars

Every piece of content should fall into one of these pillars. Aim for a balanced
mix across the week — don't lean too heavily on any single pillar.

### @carlagentileyoga (5 pillars)

**Educate (30%)** — Build authority and provide value
- Quick yoga tips and alignment cues
- "Did you know?" anatomy posts
- Breathing technique tutorials
- Common mistakes and how to fix them
- Pose breakdowns with modifications for all levels

**Inspire (20%)** — Emotional connection and motivation
- Personal yoga journey stories
- Student transformations (with permission)
- Quotes over beautiful yoga imagery
- "Why I teach" moments
- Mindfulness and intention-setting prompts

**Demonstrate (25%)** — Show expertise through practice
- Short flow sequences (Reels)
- Pose tutorials with step-by-step guidance
- Morning/evening routine demonstrations
- Desk yoga / office stretches
- Seasonal or themed practices

**Connect (15%)** — Community building and engagement
- Q&A sessions (Stories)
- Polls about practice preferences
- "Practice with me" invitations
- Behind-the-scenes of class prep
- Student shoutouts and community highlights

**Convert (10%)** — Drive to offerings
- Class/workshop announcements
- Circle community teasers (show what members get)
- Limited-time offers or early-bird pricing
- Testimonials from students/members
- "Link in bio" calls to action

### @carlagentileday (personal/lifestyle)

- Authentic day-in-the-life content
- How yoga integrates into daily life
- Family, travel, food through a wellness lens
- Cross-promote the yoga account naturally
- Personal reflections that humanize the brand

## 2. Posting Cadence & Best Times

### Recommended Weekly Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Feed posts (photo/carousel) | 3-4x per week | Core content, evergreen value |
| Reels | 4-5x per week | Highest reach and discovery |
| Stories | Daily (3-7 per day) | Keeps you top of feed, builds intimacy |
| Carousel posts | 2-3x per week | Highest saves and shares |
| Lives | 1-2x per month | Deep engagement, community feel |

### Best Times for Yoga/Wellness Niche

These are starting points — refine based on your own analytics using
`instagram_get_follower_demographics` with the `online_followers` metric.

| Day | Best Times (EST) |
|-----|-----------------|
| Monday | 6-7am, 12pm, 6-7pm |
| Tuesday | 6-7am, 12pm, 7pm |
| Wednesday | 7am, 12pm, 6pm |
| Thursday | 6-7am, 12pm, 7pm |
| Friday | 7am, 12pm, 5pm |

| Sunday | 8-9am, 12pm, 7pm |

Early morning works well because yoga practitioners often check their phones
before practice. Lunchtime catches the "I need a break" crowd. Evening catches
people planning tomorrow's practice.

## 3. Format Strategy

### When to Use Each Format

**Reels (highest priority for growth)**
- Use for: Pose tutorials, short flows, tips, trending audio, transitions
- Why: Instagram's algorithm heavily favors Reels for discovery
- Sweet spot: 15-30 seconds for maximum completion rate
- Always add captions (many watch without sound)

**Carousels (highest for saves and shares)**
- Use for: Step-by-step tutorials, myth-busting, listicles, before/after
- Why: People swipe, save, and share carousels more than single images
- Optimal: 5-7 slides with clear, readable text
- First slide must hook — treat it like a thumbnail

**Single Photos (for community and aesthetics)**
- Use for: Beautiful yoga imagery, quotes, personal moments
- Why: Clean aesthetic, great for grid planning
- Best when paired with a compelling, longer caption

**Stories (for daily connection)**
- Use for: Behind-the-scenes, polls, Q&A, quick tips, promotions
- Why: Builds daily habit of checking in, most intimate format
- Disappear in 24hr so great for time-sensitive content
- Save best ones as Highlights for new visitors

**Lives (for deep engagement)**
- Use for: Free mini-classes, Q&A sessions, guest teachers
- Why: Highest engagement, sends notifications to followers
- Schedule ahead and promote in Stories

## 4. Caption Writing Formulas

### The Hook + Value + CTA Formula

```
[Hook — first line that stops the scroll]

[Value — the meat of the post, 2-4 paragraphs]

[Call to Action — what you want them to do]

[Hashtags — in caption or first comment]
```

### Hook Templates for Yoga Content

- "The one thing most yogis get wrong about [pose]..."
- "I used to think [common belief]. Here's what changed..."
- "Stop doing [thing] in your practice. Here's why..."
- "3 signs your [body part] needs attention today"
- "This 2-minute sequence changed my mornings"
- "Your [pose] would transform if you just..."
- "I've been teaching for [X] years. This is the #1 thing I see..."
- "POV: You finally nail [challenging pose]"
- "Save this for your next practice"
- "Hot take: [controversial yoga opinion]"

### CTA Templates

**For engagement:**
- "Drop a [emoji] if this resonates"
- "Tag someone who needs this today"
- "What's your biggest struggle with [topic]? Tell me below"
- "Save this for your next practice"

**For Circle community:**
- "Want the full 30-minute version? It's in our community — link in bio"
- "My Circle members got this as a full workshop last week. Join us!"
- "We dive deeper into this inside the community every week"

**For sales:**
- "Spots are limited for [workshop] — link in bio to save yours"
- "Early bird pricing ends [date]. Don't miss this one"
- "DM me 'WORKSHOP' and I'll send you the details"

## 5. Hashtag Strategy

### Hashtag Categories (use a mix)

**Broad reach (1-2 per post):** High volume, high competition
- #yoga #yogapractice #yogainspiration #wellness #mindfulness

**Niche community (5-8 per post):** Medium volume, engaged audience
- #yogateacher #yogaforbeginners #vinyasaflow #yogaeverydamnday
- #morningyoga #yogaathome #yogaforflexibility #yogalife

**Micro/specific (5-8 per post):** Lower volume, highly targeted
- #yogateacherlife #homeyogapractice #yogaalignment
- #yogaformentalhealth #gentleyoga #yogaforall #yogaover40

**Branded (1-2 per post):**
- Create a unique hashtag like #PracticeWithCarla or #CarlaGentileYoga
- Use consistently so students can find and share their own posts

**Location-based (1-2 per post, if applicable):**
- #[city]yoga #[city]wellness #[city]yogateacher

### Hashtag Research Workflow

Use the MCP tools to research before posting:
1. `instagram_search_hashtag` — look up a hashtag ID
2. `instagram_get_hashtag_media` with type "top" — see what's performing well
3. Study the top posts: what format, caption style, engagement level?
4. Use similar hashtags but with your own unique angle

### Rules

- 20-25 hashtags per post (Instagram allows 30, but 20-25 performs better)
- Rotate hashtag sets — don't use the exact same ones every post
- Put hashtags in the caption (not first comment) for better initial reach
- Remove any hashtags that are shadowbanned or overly generic

## 6. Growth Tactics

### Organic Growth Strategies

**Collaborate with other yoga teachers**
- Collab posts (both accounts get shared audience)
- Use the `collaborators` parameter when publishing Reels
- Joint Lives or challenges

**Leverage Reels for discovery**
- Use trending audio (even just as background)
- Hook in the first 1-2 seconds
- Text overlays for accessibility
- Share Reels to Stories for double exposure

**Engagement-first approach**
- Spend 15-20 minutes before and after posting engaging with others
- Reply to every comment on your posts within the first hour
- Engage genuinely with accounts in your niche
- Use Stories polls/questions to boost engagement signals

**Content that gets shared**
- Infographics and "save-worthy" carousels
- Relatable yoga humor
- Transformation content
- "Tag a friend who..." posts

**SEO on Instagram**
- Use keywords in your username, bio, and captions
- Instagram's search now indexes caption text
- Add alt text to images for accessibility and SEO

## 7. Funnel Strategy (IG → Circle → Sales)

The goal is to move people through a journey:
**Stranger → Follower → Engaged Follower → Community Member → Customer**

### Top of Funnel (Instagram — Awareness)
- Reels for maximum reach to new audiences
- Educational carousels that get saved and shared
- Trending content that catches the algorithm

### Middle of Funnel (Instagram — Nurture)
- Stories that build daily relationship
- Longer captions that share personal stories
- Engagement through polls, Q&A, replies
- Free value that demonstrates expertise

### Bottom of Funnel (Circle — Convert)
- Tease exclusive content available in Circle
- Show behind-the-scenes of community (screenshots, testimonials)
- Limited-time offers or bonuses for new members
- "Members asked for this" content that shows community value

### Bridge Content (IG → Circle)
This is the most critical content — it needs to make followers feel like
they're missing out by not being in the community.

**What to tease on IG:**
- "Here's a 30-second preview. Full class is in the community"
- Screenshot of community conversation (with permission)
- "This week in the community, we covered..."
- Member testimonials and success stories
- Exclusive challenges that only community members can join

**Stories with link stickers:**
- "Swipe up to join our community" (or Link sticker)
- Time-sensitive: "Live Q&A in Circle today — join us"
- Polls: "Want access to my full class library? Link in bio"

## 8. Reels Best Practices

### Structure for Yoga Reels

**Tutorial Reel (15-30 sec)**
1. Hook text on screen: "Try this for tight hips"
2. Demonstrate the pose/sequence
3. Show common mistake vs. correct form
4. End with text CTA: "Save for later"

**Flow Reel (15-60 sec)**
1. Beautiful opening shot
2. Smooth transitions between poses
3. Trending or calming audio
4. Caption with the sequence name and benefits

**Talking Head Reel (15-30 sec)**
1. Hook: Make a bold statement
2. Deliver 1-2 key insights
3. CTA: "Follow for more" or "Comment if you agree"

### Technical Tips
- Film in 9:16 (vertical, full screen)
- Good lighting is non-negotiable (natural light is best for yoga)
- Steady camera or tripod
- Add closed captions (85% of people watch without sound)
- Use text overlays for key points
- Post cover image should be clear and readable as a thumbnail

### What the Algorithm Wants
- Completion rate (keep it short enough that people watch to the end)
- Shares (make it useful or relatable enough to share)
- Saves (make it reference-worthy)
- Replays (content people want to watch again)

## 9. Stories Best Practices

### Daily Story Framework

**Morning (1-2 stories)**
- Good morning / intention for the day
- Quick tip or pose of the day

**Midday (1-2 stories)**
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Poll or question sticker for engagement

**Evening (1-2 stories)**
- Recap or reflection
- Promote tomorrow's class or upcoming event
- Share a post from the feed (drives engagement)

### Story Features to Use Regularly
- **Polls**: "Morning or evening practice?"
- **Questions**: "What pose should I break down next?"
- **Quizzes**: "Which muscle does this stretch target?"
- **Countdowns**: For upcoming workshops or events
- **Link stickers**: To Circle community, class registration
- **Music**: Adds ambiance and keeps people watching

### Highlights Organization
- Welcome / About Me
- Free Tutorials
- Student Love (testimonials)
- Classes & Workshops
- Community (Circle teasers)
- Q&A Archive

## 10. Engagement & Community Building

### The 30-Minute Daily Engagement Routine

**Before posting (10 min):**
- Reply to DMs
- Respond to comments from yesterday
- Engage with 5-10 accounts in your niche (genuine comments, not generic)

**After posting (10 min):**
- Reply to every comment in the first hour (critical for algorithm)
- Share the post to Stories
- Engage with accounts who engaged with you

**Anytime (10 min):**
- Find new accounts through hashtag research
- Engage with followers' content
- Check mentions using `instagram_get_mentions`

### Comment Response Strategy
- Reply with substance, not just emojis
- Ask follow-up questions to keep conversation going
- Pin the best/most engaging comments to the top
- Heart/like all comments even if you don't reply individually

## 12. Analytics-Driven Optimization

### Weekly Review Checklist

Use the MCP tools to pull data and adjust strategy:

1. **Check account insights** (reach, impressions, follower growth)
   - `instagram_get_account_insights` with period "week"
   - Compare week-over-week

2. **Review top-performing posts**
   - `instagram_list_media` to see recent posts
   - `instagram_get_media_insights` on each to compare
   - Note: what format? what topic? what time?

3. **Check audience demographics**
   - `instagram_get_follower_demographics` — audience_gender_age, audience_city
   - Adjust content and posting times to match

4. **Review saves and shares** (these matter more than likes)
   - High saves = valuable/reference content
   - High shares = relatable/entertaining content
   - High comments = engaging/controversial content

### Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | How to Track |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| Reach growth | +5-10% weekly | Account insights |
| Engagement rate | 3-6% (yoga niche) | (likes+comments+saves+shares) / reach |
| Saves per post | Growing trend | Media insights |
| Profile visits | Growing trend | Account insights |
| Link clicks (bio) | Track separately | Link-in-bio tool analytics |
| Circle signups from IG | Track monthly | Circle member source |
| Follower growth rate | +1-3% monthly | Follower count over time |

### Monthly Audit

Once a month, review:
- Which content pillar performed best?
- Which format drives the most engagement?
- What posting times got the best reach?
- Are you converting followers to Circle members?
- What hashtags are driving discovery?

Adjust next month's strategy based on data, not assumptions.
